# Customer-Segment-Analysis
The Customer Performance Dashboard is an interactive analytics solution built in Power BI to evaluate customer demographics, behavioral patterns, and revenue contribution.

Business Objective:

The objective of this dashboard is to analyze customer demographics, behavioral segments, and revenue contribution patterns to identify high-value customer groups and support data-driven marketing and retention strategies.

This dashboard solves key business questions such as:

• Which age group drives the highest revenue? <br>
• Do customers with children contribute more revenue? <br>
• How does loyalty status impact earnings? <br>
• Is revenue evenly distributed across gender? <br>
• Who are the top-performing individual customers? <br>

The solution uses structured data modeling, advanced DAX calculations, and interactive visual exploration to transform raw customer data into actionable business insights.

Key KPIs Tracked: 

• Total Customers (18K) <br>
• Average Customer Age (46) <br>
• Revenue by Age Segment <br>
• Revenue by Gender <br>
• Customer With Children vs Without Children (% Split) <br>
• Revenue by Customer Type (Loyal, Periodic, VIP) <br>
• Top 10 High-Value Customers <br>
• Country-Level Performance for Customer Segments <br>

Tools & Technologies Used: <br>

• Power BI Desktop <br>
• Data Modeling (Star Schema Approach) <br>
• DAX (Calculated Measures & KPIs) <br>
• Data Cleaning & Transformation (Power Query)<br>
• Interactive Filters & Drill-Through Analysis<br>
• Custom Visual Formatting & UX Design<br>

Business Insights Derived:<br>

1.Core Revenue : The 30–50 age segment is the primary revenue contributor, with the 30-year age group alone accounting for a significant share of total revenue. This indicates peak earning-age customers drive business performance. <br>
2.Family-Oriented Customer Base : 72% of customers have children and generate the majority of revenue. This suggests the business is highly aligned with family-focused purchasing behavior. <br>
3.Loyalty Drives Revenue: Loyal customers contribute $21M, significantly outperforming periodic ($7M) and VIP ($1M) customers. Retention strategy directly impacts revenue stability and growth.<br>
4.Gender Revenue Balance: Revenue contribution is almost evenly distributed between male and female customers, indicating broad market appeal without heavy gender dependency.<br>
5.Revenue Concentration Risk: Top esteem customers individually generate between $125K–$145K, indicating revenue concentration among a small customer segment. Targeted retention is critical.<br>

Strategic Recommendations: <br>

• Focus marketing on 30–50 age group.<br>
• Expand loyalty programs to convert periodic customers.<br>
• Launch family-centric campaigns.<br>
• Develop personalized engagement strategies for top customers.<br>
• Use segmentation over broad demographic targeting.<br>
